The model uses only the waveform prefix ending at `pause_start`, plus the causal `pause_index` and elapsed time. It measures energy level and decay, pitch level and slope, voicing continuity, spectral shape, active-speech runs, gaps, and syllable-like energy peaks. It combines a sparse logistic model, randomized trees, and histogram gradient boosting, with separate English and Hindi ensembles and a generic fallback. Pause index and elapsed time act as a causal hazard prior rather than revealing whether a pause is the final annotation. Long hold pauses receive more training weight because those are the pauses that can interrupt a user at the scorer's operating delays. Grouped validation splits complete turns and reached 1250 ms on English and 802 ms on Hindi. The model still fails on continuation pauses that have sentence-final falling prosody and on true endings with weak or clipped final speech. With one more day, I would manually listen to the listed out-of-fold errors, add speaker-normalized phrase-final lengthening features, and tune the ensemble directly with nested grouped validation.
